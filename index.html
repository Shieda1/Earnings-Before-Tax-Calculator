What is the pre-tax income?
the pre-tax income (EBT) is the remaining earnings after all operating and non operating expenses except for the taxes have been accounted for.
How to calculate pre-tax income?
To calculate pre-tax income you should use the following formula:
pre-tax income (EBT) = operating income (EBIT) - interest expense
where:
EBIT = revenue - cost of goods sold (COGS) - operating expenses
Example: company x has $13,000,000 in revenue, $6,000,000 in cost of goods sold, $2,300,000 of operating expenses and $670,000 worth of interest expense, calculate the pre-tax income?
First we should calculate the EBIT:
EBIT = revenue - cost of goods sold - operating expenses
EBIT = $13,000,000 - $6,000,000 - $2,300,000
EBIT = $4,700,000
now let's calculate the pre-tax income:
pre-tax income = operating income - interest expense
pre-tax income = $4,700,000 - $670,000
pre-tax income = $4,030,000
Why use pre-tax income?
The pre-tax income is used because it provides an insight into the firm's financial performance before the impact of taxes. It is also used to measure the business performance and health.
